About

My work is positioned at the intersection of digital design, verification, embedded systems and computer architecture. I am especially interested in building practical FPGA-based platforms that make advanced computer architecture ideas measurable, reproducible and closer to real hardware.

Engineering background

Experience with ASIC/FPGA development, SoC integration, verification environments, hardware/software co-design, DSP-oriented systems and FPGA-based prototypes.

Research profile

PhD research focused on Near-Memory Computing and FPGA-based evaluation platforms for data-intensive workloads and memory-centric architectures.

Research

My current research investigates how FPGA prototypes can be used to explore Processing-Near-Memory architectures, bridging the gap between high-level simulation and deployable hardware platforms.

Near-Memory Computing

Exploration of architectures that reduce data movement by moving computation closer to memory resources.

FPGA Prototyping

Construction of configurable FPGA-based systems for architectural exploration, instrumentation and experimental evaluation.

Hardware/Software Integration

Workflows involving embedded software, memory maps, DMA engines, control interfaces, drivers, simulation and FPGA deployment.

Selected projects
